# The Global AI Shift

AI is not only a product trend. It is becoming part of how markets compete, how states invest, and how critical sectors modernise. Leaders therefore need to treat AI as a strategic shift in infrastructure, capability, and power.

## Why the Shift Matters

AI is moving from isolated pilots toward embedded capability across software, operations, and public services. As adoption widens, competitive advantage increasingly depends on who can deploy AI responsibly at scale, not merely who can test the latest model first.

## Core Themes in This Chapter

- **Industry adoption trends**: where AI use is accelerating, and where adoption remains constrained by data, regulation, or workflow complexity
- **AI as strategic infrastructure**: how compute, data, models, and platforms are becoming part of long-term organisational capability
- **Geopolitical and economic implications**: how national strategy, supply chains, and global competition shape AI access and risk
- **AI in critical sectors**: why healthcare, finance, telecom, manufacturing, and public services require a higher standard of reliability and oversight

## Leadership Lens

For executives, the important question is not whether AI is globally significant in the abstract. It is how that shift changes investment timing, dependency risk, talent needs, and competitive expectations in their own sector.

## Key Questions for Leaders

- Where is AI changing the basis of competition in our market?
- Which external dependencies could constrain our AI ambitions?
- Which sector-specific adoption patterns matter more than general market headlines?
