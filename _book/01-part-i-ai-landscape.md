# Part I — Understanding the AI Landscape

This part establishes the external context for the rest of the book: what AI is, why the environment has changed so quickly, and where legal and financial exposure now begins.

It should be read as a sequence. The first chapter clarifies what leaders are actually dealing with when people say "AI." The second explains why AI has become a strategic shift in markets, infrastructure, and state policy rather than just another software trend. The third translates that shift into regulatory, legal, and financial consequences.

The practical aim is simple: before leaders decide where to invest, they need a clear view of the technology, the market structure around it, and the external constraints that are already taking shape.
