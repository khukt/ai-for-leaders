Looks Strong

Accumulation is not the same as advantage

Large volumes of records can look impressive while still being fragmented, weakly governed, and difficult to reuse in a live workflow.

What It Looks Like

Large stores of records, documents, logs, or reports that exist across systems but are hard to combine, explain, or operationalize.

What Leaders Misread

Volume is often mistaken for strength even when rights, quality, and workflow fit are still weak.

What It Really Means

The organisation may have raw material, but not yet a reusable capability.

What To Do

Prioritize access, definitions, lineage, and workflow relevance before claiming strategic advantage.

Key idea: data becomes strategic only when it is usable, governable, and tied to an important workflow outcome.

Accumulation More records than usable capability.

Foundation Basic access and stewardship exist.

Advantage Workflow-relevant and hard to copy.

The data improves an important workflow and can be reused with confidence.

Debt Weakness is already embedded.
