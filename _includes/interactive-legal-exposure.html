Use Case Exposure

Exposure starts with what the system is being asked to do

Legal burden changes sharply when AI affects consequential decisions rather than low-stakes drafting or support work.

What Creates Exposure

Hiring, pricing, access, eligibility, safety, and rights-affecting decisions create much higher legal sensitivity.

What Leaders Miss

Teams often treat all AI use as one category and miss how quickly obligations change when the workflow becomes consequential.

What To Check Now

Ask which rights, decisions, or business outcomes the system can influence and whether that use has been formally approved.

Leadership Move

Separate low-stakes assistance from high-impact decision support before governance, procurement, and review all blur together.

Key idea: legal exposure usually starts in the workflow, data, and governance choices around the system before it appears as a formal compliance event.

Data Data obligations arrive early.

Personal data, sensitive data, and cross-border movement can create exposure before model quality is even debated.

Vendor Buying the tool does not transfer accountability.

Model providers can reduce build effort without absorbing deployment, oversight, or customer-facing risk.

Documentation Weak records become a legal weakness.

If approvals, limits, and monitoring are not visible, the organisation will struggle to defend how the system was used.

Public Impact Scrutiny rises when people feel the consequences.

Customer-facing and employee-facing systems can turn local failures into broader reputational and regulatory problems.
