EU AI Act: risk tier, obligations, and timing

Select a risk tier to see what the Act does to that category. The main point is that the law is not one flat rulebook: different uses trigger very different obligations.

Unacceptable Risk

Some AI uses are prohibited outright

The AI Act bans certain uses because the risk is considered incompatible with EU law and values rather than something to be managed through controls alone.

Typical Examples

Social scoring, certain manipulative uses, and several prohibited biometric practices.

Legal Effect

The right response is not stronger documentation. It is to avoid the prohibited practice.

What Management Needs

Clear screening of use cases before deployment and a process to escalate anything that may fall into a banned category.

Leadership Question

Are we trying to govern something that the Act treats as impermissible in the first place?

Phased dates
Aug 1, 2024

Act entered into force

Feb 2, 2025

Prohibited practices and AI literacy duties apply

Aug 2, 2025

Governance and GPAI model obligations apply

Aug 2, 2026

Most of the Act becomes fully applicable

Aug 2, 2027

Longer timetable for some regulated high-risk products

Key idea: the AI Act is a tiered regime. The harder the potential impact, the stronger the obligations, evidence, and governance burden.
