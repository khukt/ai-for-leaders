Choosing the right sourcing path

Choose a sourcing path to see where it fits, what it gives you, what leaders usually underestimate, and what should be checked before approval.

Highest Control

Build when control is genuinely strategic and operationally supportable

Building makes sense when success depends heavily on proprietary context, internal differentiation, or assurance requirements you cannot comfortably externalize.

Pause Condition

If the value thesis, ownership, or governance case is still weak, the right sourcing choice may be not to source yet.

Best Fit

Core capabilities, distinctive workflows, proprietary data advantage, or uses with demanding assurance and control needs.

What You Gain

Greater control over adaptation, standards, governance, and long-term capability development.

What Leaders Underestimate

Building is not only software creation. It is an ongoing operating commitment across evaluation, monitoring, incident handling, staffing, and change control.

What To Check Before Approval

Verify that the need for control is real, the use case matters enough, and the organisation can support the capability after launch.

Key idea: the right sourcing choice depends less on technical preference and more on the balance between control, speed, dependency, and operating burden.

Build Use when control is strategic.

Best for distinctive, high-control capabilities, but only if the organisation can operate them sustainably.

Buy Use when speed and replaceability matter.

Best for common capabilities, but still requires disciplined vendor governance and exit planning.

Partner Use when value sits across boundaries.

Best for shared infrastructure or capability, but only when governance, IP, and decision rights are designed up front.

Pause Condition

If the problem, economics, or governance case is unclear, the best sourcing choice may be not to source yet.

Figure: build, buy, and partner are different long-term commitments, not just different delivery tactics.
