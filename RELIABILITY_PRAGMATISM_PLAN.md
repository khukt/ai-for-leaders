# Reliability and Pragmatism Improvement Plan

This plan translates the current manuscript review into a practical editing sequence. The goal is to make the book more defensible, more usable, and more consistent as an executive guide.

## Current Assessment

- **Strongest chapters**: 03, 05, 09, 10, 11, and 16
- **Main reliability gap**: uneven maturity and uneven sourcing across the full manuscript
- **Main pragmatism gap**: several late chapters and appendices are still too abstract to function as executive tools

## Priority 1: Complete the Practical Backbone

### 12. Compliance and Audit Readiness
- Add a concrete evidence pack
- Define minimum documentation expectations
- Add third-party assurance and ongoing monitoring sections

### 17. The Executive Five-Year Agenda
- Replace generic framing with a timed action agenda
- Separate immediate priorities from medium-term capability building
- Add board-level questions and ownership logic

### Appendices A-D
- Convert placeholders into real checklists, templates, and comparison tools
- Ensure each appendix can be used in a meeting without further rewriting

## Priority 2: Strengthen Thin Strategy Chapters

### 07. Build, Buy, or Partner
- Add vendor diligence criteria
- Add exit risk and hidden-cost analysis
- Add decision prompts for strategic versus commoditized capability

### 08. Data as a Strategic Asset
- Distinguish operational, training, retrieval, and audit data more explicitly
- Add data-readiness checks
- Add a short evidence and traceability model

## Priority 3: Strengthen Future and Transformation Chapters

### 13. Leading Organizational Transformation
- Add workforce transition patterns
- Add decision-rights redesign
- Add trust and leadership communication guidance

### 14. Human-AI Collaboration and Autonomous Systems
- Add a collaboration spectrum
- Add autonomy boundaries and intervention triggers
- Add governance criteria for expanding autonomy

### 15. AI in Smart Industry and Critical Infrastructure
- Add assurance tiers
- Add operational readiness criteria
- Add sector examples with failure consequences

## Priority 4: Source and Citation Upgrade

- Keep practical explainer material as an interpretive aid, not the main authority
- Increase the weight of OECD, NIST, EU, and other primary sources where substantive claims are made
- Reduce unsupported statements in thinner chapters

## Editing Standard

For each chapter, require:

- one clear leadership question
- one concrete decision framework, checklist, or operating model
- enough sourcing to support material claims
- a stronger distinction between principle, action, and evidence

1. Chapter 12
2. Chapter 17
3. Appendices A-D
4. Chapter 07
5. Chapter 08
6. Chapter 13
7. Chapter 14
8. Chapter 15
